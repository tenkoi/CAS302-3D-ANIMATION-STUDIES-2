▣ MISSION BRIEFING — ABOUT THIS SUBJECT

CAS302 – 3D Animation Studies 2 takes you deeper into the world of 3D motion. Across 6 weeks you will explore physics and motion in 3D, camera and object animation, 3D motion graphics and animated typography, visual effects, and rendering with basic compositing — all in Blender 4.5 LTS and above.

The course builds practical production skills and critical understanding of how animated imagery is created, shared and sold in today's visual economy — preparing you for employment or further study.

BLENDER OPEN MOVIES — HOW A FREE TOOL POWERS REAL STUDIOS

Blender Studio has produced open short films such as Spring, Sprite Fright and Charge entirely in Blender, releasing all production files publicly. These projects prove that the same free software you are learning is used in professional pipelines.

  • Discuss: why does file/folder organisation matter from day one of a production?

◈ CASE FILE 02

THE ONE-SHOT ILLUSION — CONTINUOUS CAMERA IN FILM & GAMES

Films like 1917 and games like God of War (2018) famously use "one-shot" continuous camera movement to pull the audience through the story without visible cuts. Virtual cameras make this achievable in 3D animation in ways real cameras never could.

  • Study a one-shot sequence: where does the camera dolly, orbit, and push in — and why?
  • Identify moments where the camera motion hides what would have been a cut (CLO1).
  • Discuss: when is a cut stronger than a continuous move? Find one example of each.

★ BOSS QUEST — WEEK 2

Build a small scene and film it with a 3-shot camera sequence: one orbit (Follow Path), one push-in, and one cut to a second camera using markers. Minimum 10 seconds.

◈ CASE FILE 03

CHAIN REACTIONS — OK GO'S RUBE GOLDBERG MACHINE

OK Go's music video This Too Shall Pass features a giant real-world Rube Goldberg machine — a masterclass in cause-and-effect timing. In 3D, rigid body simulation lets you build the same chain reactions digitally, with full control over mass, friction and momentum.

  • Watch the chain reaction: identify five moments of energy transfer between objects (CLO2).
  • Which parts would be easy in Blender's rigid body system, and which would need keyframed "cheats"?
  • Discuss: why does slightly imperfect motion feel more believable than perfect motion?

★ BOSS QUEST — WEEK 3

Create a domino chain reaction: at least 20 dominoes, one trigger object, correct active/passive setup, baked simulation, and polished easing on any keyframed motion. This is your warm-up for Assignment 1 Part B.

◈ CASE FILE 04

TITLES THAT TELL STORIES — FROM SAUL BASS TO THE UN SDG CAMPAIGN

Great title sequences — from Saul Bass's classic film openings to modern series intros — compress a whole story into seconds of moving type. The United Nations' SDG campaign materials show how motion graphics carry a global message through colour, icons and typography.

  • Analyse one title sequence: how do type choice, timing and colour set the tone? (CLO4)
  • Study the official SDG colour system and icons — how would you translate one goal into motion?
  • Discuss: what makes a title card "resolve" feel satisfying at the end of a sequence?

★ BOSS QUEST — WEEK 4

Design a 10-second animated title card for your favourite SDG: 3D text with bevels, one entrance animation, one camera move, refined in the Graph Editor. This becomes your Final Project style test.

◈ CASE FILE 05

ONE-PERSON VFX — IAN HUBERT'S DYNAMO DREAM

Ian Hubert's Dynamo Dream series is a landmark of solo VFX production — feature-quality sci-fi worlds, particles, smoke and compositing built almost entirely in Blender by a tiny team. It shows what your Assignment 1 Part B techniques scale up to.

  • Watch a Dynamo Dream episode or breakdown: spot three simulated effects (particles, smoke, dynamics) (CLO3).
  • How does compositing "glue" the CG elements to the footage? Look for grade, grain and glow.
  • Discuss: with limited render time, where would you spend your simulation budget in a 15-second shot?

★ BOSS QUEST — WEEK 5

Produce a 5-second VFX test shot combining TWO techniques (e.g., particles + smoke, or rigid body + particles). Bake your caches properly. This directly feeds Assignment 1 Part B.

◈ CASE FILE 06

EEVEE VS CYCLES IN PRODUCTION — SPRITE FRIGHT & REAL-TIME MOGRAPH

Blender Studio's Sprite Fright was rendered in Cycles for full path-traced quality, while much of today's commercial motion graphics ships straight out of Eevee for speed. Choosing an engine is a production decision: quality vs deadline vs style.

  • Compare a Cycles frame and an Eevee frame of the same scene: list three visible differences (CLO5).
  • For your Final Project title animation, which engine fits your style and schedule — and why?
  • Discuss: how do denoising and sample counts change the render-time equation for a 60-second piece?

★ FINAL BOSS — WEEK 6

Render your Week 4 title card at 1920×1080, 24/25 fps, MP4 (H.264) — once in Eevee, once in Cycles — then add a compositor grade with glow and vignette. Compare the results. GAME COMPLETE: you are ready for the Final Project.
